ACME Corporation Employee Handbook
Version 2024.1

Chapter 1: Time Off and Leave Policies

All full-time employees receive 20 days of paid vacation per year. Vacation days accrue at a rate of 1.67 days per month. Unused vacation days may be carried over to the next year, up to a maximum of 5 days. Employees must request vacation at least two weeks in advance through the HR portal.

Sick leave is provided at 10 days per year. Sick leave does not accrue or carry over. For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days, a doctor's note is required. Employees may use sick leave for their own illness or to care for an immediate family member.

Parental leave is available for all new parents. Birth parents receive 16 weeks of paid leave. Non-birth parents receive 8 weeks of paid leave. Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or adoption.

Chapter 2: Remote Work Policy

ACME Corporation supports a hybrid work model. Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Core office days are Tuesday and Thursday, when all team members are expected to be in the office.

Remote work requires a stable internet connection and a dedicated workspace. The company provides a one-time stipend of $500 for home office setup. Employees working remotely must be available during core hours of 9 AM to 3 PM in their local time zone.

International remote work is permitted for up to 4 weeks per year with prior approval from HR and the employee's manager. Tax implications of international remote work are the responsibility of the employee.

Chapter 3: Compensation and Benefits

Salary reviews are conducted annually in March. Performance ratings directly influence salary adjustments. The company targets the 75th percentile of market compensation for all roles.

Health insurance is provided through BlueCross BlueShield. The company covers 90% of employee premiums and 70% of dependent premiums. Dental and vision coverage are included in all plans. Open enrollment occurs in November each year.

The company offers a 401(k) retirement plan with a 4% employer match. Employees are eligible to participate after 90 days of employment. The match vests over a 3-year period.

An Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP) is available, allowing employees to purchase company stock at a 15% discount through payroll deductions.

Chapter 4: Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional behavior. Harassment, discrimination, and retaliation in any form are strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to the employee's manager and the legal department. Outside employment is permitted as long as it does not interfere with the employee's responsibilities at ACME Corporation.

Confidential information must be protected at all times. Employees must not share proprietary information, trade secrets, or customer data with unauthorized parties. All employees must complete annual security awareness training.

Chapter 5: Expense Policy

Business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Flights should be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible. Economy class is standard for domestic flights; business class may be approved for international flights exceeding 6 hours.

Hotel accommodations should not exceed $200 per night in standard markets and $300 per night in high-cost markets such as New York, San Francisco, and London.

Meals during business travel are reimbursed up to $75 per day. Alcohol is not reimbursable. All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of the trip through the expense management system with appropriate receipts.

Chapter 6: Professional Development

Each employee receives an annual professional development budget of $2,000. This may be used for conferences, courses, certifications, and books. Requests must be approved by the employee's manager.

The company offers internal mentorship programs and quarterly learning sessions. Employees are encouraged to spend up to 10% of their work time on learning and professional growth.

Tuition reimbursement is available for degree programs related to the employee's role, up to $5,250 per year. Employees must maintain a grade of B or higher and commit to remaining with the company for at least one year after completing the program.
